# Case Study Anchor: Target Inventory Failure (2022)

In 2022, Target publicly reported significant inventory imbalances following a rapid shift in consumer demand. Categories that performed well during the pandemic experienced declining demand, while inventory levels remained high due to earlier purchasing decisions and long lead times.

As a result, Target faced:
- Excess inventory and aggressive markdowns
- Margin pressure
- Supplier renegotiations and operational adjustments

This situation highlights a common retail planning challenge: decisions are often made based on outdated demand assumptions, with limited mechanisms to detect forecast bias early or stress-test inventory policies under changing conditions.

---

## Why this case is relevant
The Target inventory issue was not caused by poor execution alone, but by planning decisions made under uncertainty. It illustrates the importance of:
- Monitoring forecast bias, not just accuracy
- Adjusting inventory policies dynamically
- Understanding how service-level targets affect inventory risk
- Stress-testing plans under alternative demand scenarios

This project uses the Target case as a real-world anchor to design a planning system that could help mitigate similar failures in large retail organizations.
